Deepfake technology has evolved into one of the most significant challenges in digital security, misinformation control, and media authenticity. TRUTHLENS is designed as a full-stack deepfake detection ecosystem combining machine learning, web deployment, and explainability.

1. Introduction

Deepfakes use neural networks such as Autoencoders and GANs to generate manipulated images and videos that appear real. Their misuse ranges from misinformation, political manipulation, financial scams, and identity impersonation. Therefore, a reliable AI-powered detection pipeline is critical.

4. Methodology

The system processes uploaded media (image/video), applies model inference, and then outputs class prediction with confidence.

4.1 Dataset Preparation

  • ~90,000 total frames
  • Filtered corrupted/short-length videos
  • Train/Val/Test split maintained

4.2 Image Classification (CNN)

ResNet18 extracts deep spatial features such as texture inconsistencies, blending artifacts, and unnatural facial distortions.

4.3 Video Classification

Videos are sampled for 30 frames ‚Üí each frame classified ‚Üí average probability determines the final label.

Modeling (PyTorch)

  • Frame sampling for video inference
